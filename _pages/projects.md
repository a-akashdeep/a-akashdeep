# Projects

This page presents selected research projects based on my publication record. Each project connects several publications around a common scientific question, method, or material system.

## Project 1: Spin transport and interface effects in RuO₂/permalloy heterostructures

**Summary:**  
This project investigates angle-dependent magnetoresistance and spin-current-related effects in RuO₂/permalloy heterostructures.

**Research question:**  
How can we distinguish intrinsic altermagnetic contributions from interface-generated spin-current effects in RuO₂-based heterostructures?

**Methods:**  
Magnetotransport, angle-dependent magnetoresistance, temperature-dependent measurements, magnetic-field-dependent measurements, crystallographic-orientation-dependent analysis.

**Key outcome:**  
The work shows that interface-generated spin currents at the RuO₂/permalloy interface can dominate the observed magnetoresistance and must be carefully separated from possible intrinsic altermagnetic contributions.

**Related publication:**  
Akashdeep et al., “Angle-dependent magnetoresistance induced by interface-generated spin current in RuO₂/permalloy heterostructures,” Physical Review Applied, 2025.

## Project 2: Surface-localized magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films

**Summary:**  
This project uses low-energy muon probes to study the spatial distribution of magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films.

**Research question:**  
Is the magnetic response in RuO₂ thin films an intrinsic bulk property, or can it originate from surface-localized regions?

**Methods:**  
Low-energy muon spin rotation/relaxation, depth-sensitive magnetic characterization, thin-film growth comparison, temperature-dependent measurements.

**Key outcome:**  
The study provides evidence for surface-confined, inhomogeneous magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films, emphasizing the importance of surfaces, defects, strain, and reduced dimensionality.

**Related publication:**  
Akashdeep et al., “Surface-localized magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films revealed by low-energy muon probes,” Applied Physics Letters, 2026.

## Project 3: Experimental signatures of altermagnetism in RuO₂ and hematite

**Summary:**  
This project area focuses on identifying experimental signatures of altermagnetic order using spectroscopic, imaging, and transport methods.

**Research question:**  
How can altermagnetic order be experimentally detected through band-structure, dichroism, imaging, and anomalous transport signatures?

**Methods:**  
Spin-resolved spectroscopy, MCD-XPS, XMCD imaging, XPEEM, anomalous Hall transport, collaborative comparison with theory.

**Key outcome:**  
These studies contribute to the experimental identification of altermagnetic order and symmetry-driven transport in materials including RuO₂ and hematite.

**Related publications:**  
Fedchenko et al., Science Advances, 2024.  
Lytvynenko et al., Physical Review B, 2026.  
Galíndez-Ruales et al., Advanced Materials, 2025.

## Project 4: Magnon transport in altermagnetic LuFeO₃

**Summary:**  
This project investigates non-local magnon transport in a d-wave altermagnet.

**Research question:**  
Can altermagnetic symmetry enable direction-dependent, field-free magnon spin transport?

**Methods:**  
Spin Seebeck measurements, spin Hall injection and detection, non-local transport, atomistic spin dynamics, spin-wave theory collaboration.

**Key outcome:**  
The work reports direction-dependent magnon transport in LuFeO₃, highlighting the potential of altermagnets for low-power magnonic and spintronic applications.

**Related publication:**  
Galíndez-Ruales et al., “Altermagnetic magnon transport in the d-wave altermagnet LuFeO₃,” arXiv, 2025.

## Project 5: Spin dynamics in ferrimagnetic garnet heterostructures

**Summary:**  
This project studies spin-current generation and magnetization dynamics in ferrimagnetic garnet/heavy-metal heterostructures.

**Research question:**  
How do coupled ferrimagnetic layers and compensation effects influence spin dynamics and spin-current generation?

**Methods:**  
Ferromagnetic resonance, spin pumping, spin Seebeck effect, inverse spin Hall detection, temperature-dependent measurements, micromagnetic simulations.

**Key outcome:**  
The work shows how coupled ferrimagnetic systems such as YIG/GdIG/Pt can act as tunable spin-current sources and provide access to spin dynamics near compensation regimes.

**Related publications:**  
Fuhrmann et al., Physical Review B, 2025.  
Loyal et al., arXiv, 2026.
