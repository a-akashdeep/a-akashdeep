# Research

My research lies at the intersection of experimental condensed matter physics, spintronics, magnetic thin films, and emerging magnetic materials. I am especially interested in how magnetic order, crystal symmetry, interfaces, surfaces, and spin-orbit coupling influence transport and dynamics in magnetic heterostructures.

## 1. Altermagnetism and RuO₂-based systems

A major part of my research focuses on RuO₂ and related altermagnetic systems. Altermagnets are compensated magnetic materials that can show unconventional spin-dependent electronic and transport properties despite having little or no net magnetization.

In my work, I have investigated RuO₂-based heterostructures using magnetotransport and advanced characterization methods. This includes studying interface-generated spin currents in RuO₂/permalloy heterostructures and surface-localized magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films using low-energy muon probes.

Related topics:

- RuO₂ thin films
- Altermagnetic spin transport
- Interface-generated spin currents
- Surface-localized magnetism
- Magnetotransport and angle-dependent magnetoresistance
- Low-energy muon spin rotation/relaxation

## 2. Experimental signatures of altermagnetism

My publication record also includes collaborative work on identifying altermagnetic behavior using advanced experimental techniques such as spin- and angle-resolved spectroscopy, magnetic circular dichroism, XMCD imaging, and anomalous Hall transport.

These studies help connect crystal symmetry, magnetic order, and measurable transport or spectroscopic signatures in materials such as RuO₂, hematite, and LuFeO₃.

Related topics:

- Time-reversal symmetry breaking in RuO₂
- MCD-XPS and XMCD in altermagnetic films
- XMCD imaging and anomalous Hall transport in hematite
- Direction-dependent magnon transport in LuFeO₃

## 3. Spin dynamics and magnonics in ferrimagnetic heterostructures

Another important research direction is spin dynamics and spin-current generation in ferrimagnetic insulator/heavy-metal systems. This includes work on YIG/GdIG/Pt trilayers and TbIG/Pt heterostructures, where compensation effects and non-collinear magnetic phases can strongly influence spin-current signals.

Related topics:

- Ferromagnetic resonance
- Spin pumping
- Spin Seebeck effect
- Inverse spin Hall detection
- Compensation temperatures
- Magnon transport
- Topological Hall-like responses
